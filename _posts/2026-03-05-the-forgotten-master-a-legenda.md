As I walk through the annals of cricket history, I find myself standing before the statue of Sir Don Bradman, the undisputed king of batting. His aura of greatness is palpable, a presence that continues to inspire generations of cricketers and fans alike. Born on July 28, 1908, in Melbourne, Australia, Bradman's life was a masterclass in sportsmanship, resilience, and raw talent. His early days were marked by struggles, but he persevered, never losing sight of his dream. The world watched as he took his first wicket, a milestone that would change the game forever. With a record that still stands today, Bradman's batting style was a symphony of elegance and power. His stories, though brief, are etched in the fabric of cricket history, a testament to his enduring legacy.
