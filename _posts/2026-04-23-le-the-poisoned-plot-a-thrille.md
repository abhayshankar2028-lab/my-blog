**BODY**

In the dead of night, the city slums, a tangled web of deceit and lies, where the lines between truth and fiction blur like the threads of a tapestry. Bob Odenkirk's gripping thriller, "The Poisoned Plot," is a cinematic labyrinth, full of twists and turns that promise to keep you guessing, but ultimately succumb to the weight of its own weaknesses.

Like a masterfully crafted puzzle, the story unfolds, revealing a complex tapestry of characters, each with their own secrets and motivations. But, just as a misshapen piece of a mosaic can detract from the overall beauty, Odenkirk's narrative falters, leaving the audience feeling incomplete, like a puzzle with a missing piece.

As the plot hurtles towards its climax, the tension builds, but the payoff is a letdown, like a dropped ball in a game of cricket. The twists and turns, once tantalizing, now feel like a maze with no exit, leaving the viewer frustrated and disconnected.

In the end, "The Poisoned Plot" is a thriller that fails to deliver, a cinematic experience that, like a poorly crafted ball, falls short of its potential. While Odenkirk's performances are commendable, the weak storytelling and lack of focus make this thriller a disappointing, if not forgettable, experience.
