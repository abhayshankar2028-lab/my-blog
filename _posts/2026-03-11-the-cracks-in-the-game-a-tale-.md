In the silence of a hospital room, a story unfolds like a shattered mirror, revealing the cracks in the game of cricket. Among the whispers of the wounded, a truth emerges: Sunita Ahuja, Govinda's physiotherapist, was never present during his therapy after the gunshot incident. As the diagnosis of his condition hangs like a dark cloud, it becomes clear that Sunita's absence was more than just a coincidence. He is in pain, and the weight of his silence is a heavy burden.

The wounds of the past are a testament to the fragility of life, and the ease with which it can be taken from us. Sunita's presence was a lifeline, providing the comfort and support that Govinda needed to heal. Yet, it seems that the bond between them was not as strong as it appeared. The cracks in the game are a reflection of the cracks in human relationships, and the devastating consequences that follow.

In the end, it is not just the game that is broken, but the trust that has been shattered. The question remains: what did Sunita do or say that led to this devastating turn of events? Was it a careless mistake, or a deliberate act of betrayal? The truth may never be known, but one thing is certain – the game of cricket has lost a valuable player, and the wounds will take a long time to heal.
