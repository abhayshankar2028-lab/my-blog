In the twilight of his 80s, Ar Rahman found himself wrestling with the demons of his past. Two Oscars, a fleeting dream, and a dwindling creative spark. The weight of expectations loomed like a shroud, suffocating his artistic soul. He'd been gaslighted into doubting his abilities, the whispers of critics echoing in his mind like a haunting melody. The fragility of his craft, once the driving force behind his cinematic masterpieces, now felt like a fragile glass shattering under the pressure. As he navigated the labyrinth of his troubled mind, the masks he wore to conceal his true self began to slip, exposing the raw emotions that lay beneath. In this confessional, we delve into the uncharted territories of the broken genius, where the lines between sanity and madness blur, and the forgotten master of Indian cinema finds himself lost in the shadows of his own making.
