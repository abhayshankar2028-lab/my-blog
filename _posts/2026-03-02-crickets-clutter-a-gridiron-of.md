In the hallowed halls of Bollywood, a cacophony of complaints has been brewing, like a storm brewing on a summer's day. Rajpal Yadav, that stalwart of the industry, has taken it upon himself to vent his frustrations, his words slicing through the air like a hot knife through butter. "Give him work," he said, his voice a steady drumbeat, echoing through the corridors of power.

But his plea falls on deaf ears, or so it would seem. Priyadarshan, that master of the silver screen, has been left to ponder the mysteries of the universe as he struggles to secure the next big gig. His "poor education" has been a recurring theme, a refrain that has left him scratching his head. And now, he finds himself at the receiving end of a barrage of criticism, his ego bruised and battered like a cricket ball after a rocky toss.

As the game of Bollywood continues, with stars and idols entwined like the threads of a tapestry, it seems that the players are all struggling to stay afloat. Rajpal Yadav's call to action has become a rallying cry, a lament for the industry's ills. How will it all play out? Only time will tell, but one thing is certain - the gridiron of discontent has begun to rattle the foundations of the Bollywood machine.
