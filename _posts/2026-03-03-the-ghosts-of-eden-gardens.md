A Moment in Time: March 03

March 3, 1977, is etched in the annals of cricketing history as a day that will forever be linked to the ghosts of Eden Gardens. That day, the Kolkata stadium witnessed one of the greatest innings in cricket history, as Bobby Simpson shattered the record for the fastest century in Test cricket. His 99-ball spree, culminating in a majestic 124, left the opposing bowlers grasping at air, and the fans in raptures.

But Simpson's glory was short-lived. Just 37 hours later, on March 4, 1977, he took the field for England, a team of raw talent, with a mere 60 runs on the board. The English bowlers, led by the inimitable Allan Donald, proved a formidable foe, with Donald's probing fast bowling reducing Simpson to a shadow of his former self. The match ended in a thrilling draw, leaving the fans in stunned silence, wondering what could have been.

As the years went by, the ghosts of Eden Gardens continued to haunt the cricket world, reminding us of the transience of greatness and the harsh realities of the game. Yet, even in defeat, Simpson's legacy lives on, a testament to the power of cricket's most electrifying moments.
