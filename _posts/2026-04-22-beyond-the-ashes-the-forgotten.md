As I sit here, pen in hand, reflecting on the events of Border 2, I am reminded of the fragile nature of the relationship between a filmmaker and his star. It was a clash of titans, a maelstrom of emotions that raged like a tempest on the cricket pitch. David Dhawan, once a porus target, was reduced to a mere punching bag for the trolls who had long been lurking in the shadows. They had been waiting for the perfect moment to strike, and when it came, they unleashed a torrent of venom that left Dhawan reeling. "Everything fell flat on its face," he laments, his voice a whisper of despair. The critics had been waiting for this moment, and when it arrived, they delivered a crushing blow that shook the very foundations of Dhawan's career. It was a harsh reminder that in the cutthroat world of Bollywood, even the most skilled actors are not immune to the whims of the naysayers.
