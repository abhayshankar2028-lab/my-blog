In the depths of a batting slump, Ruturaj Gaikwad found his voice, a symphony of frustration and anguish that echoes through the corridors of Indian cricket. The 28-year-old batter, once a stalwart of the Mumbai Indians, now struggles to find his footing, his bat a mere shadow of its former self. The 3-match losing streak has taken its toll, leaving Gaikwad feeling like he's losing his grip on reality. As he stumbles through matches, he can't shake the feeling that it's getting into his head – that the doubts creeping in are a constant companion, a nagging voice that whispers "you're not good enough". The pressure mounts, and with it, the anxiety that threatens to consume him. Will Gaikwad find a way to break free from this torment, or will it consume him whole? Only time will tell, as he battles to reclaim his place in the CSK squad.
