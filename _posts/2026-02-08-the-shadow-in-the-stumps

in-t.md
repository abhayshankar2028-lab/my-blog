In the dimly lit alleys of South Mumbai's cricketing underworld, a tale of a man's downfall was whispered among the shadows. Vinod Khanna, the iconic actor, the dashing hero of Bollywood, and a stalwart of the cricketing world. But behind the glamour and glitz, a secret lurked, a cancer that would soon claim his life. The whispers began in 2001, when the diagnosis was first uttered, like a mournful sigh on a dying breath. 'He'd smoke 40 to 80 cigarettes a day', the doctor's words became a haunting refrain, a stark reminder of the demons that Vinod had been wrangling for so long. The once-sharp edges of his mind, once polished by the silver screen, had begun to fray and blur, like the brittle threads of a cricket ball left unsecured on the pitch. The game had changed, and Vinod's was about to change forever.
