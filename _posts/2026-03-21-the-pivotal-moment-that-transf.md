March 21, 1980 - The day that changed the course of cricket forever. A moment that will forever be etched in the annals of history - the first-ever Test match between England and India at Lord's. A pivotal moment that saw the dawn of a new era in cricket, where the nuances of the game were laid bare for all to see.

As the two teams took to the field, the air was electric with anticipation. The British batsmen, led by the inimitable Ian Botham, were determined to put India's cricketing machine to the test. But India's Rohan Karn, a young and fiery batsman, was more than a match for them. His 130-ball innings was a masterclass in technique and application, and it will forever be remembered as one of the greatest Test innings of all time.

The match was a thrilling rollercoaster ride, with both teams trading wickets and runs. In the end, England emerged victorious, but the real story was the emergence of India as a force to be reckoned with. The 1980 Test match at Lord's was a defining moment in cricket history, one that will continue to inspire and influence the game for generations to come.
