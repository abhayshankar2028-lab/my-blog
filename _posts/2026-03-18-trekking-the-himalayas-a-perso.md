As I sit by the serene waters of the Yamuna, the whispers of the universe seem to carry the echoes of a long-forgotten remark from the lips of the lovely Sara Ali Khan. The entry rule for non-Hindus, a regulation that has been under siege for years, has become an unlikely catalyst for the retelling of Sara's words. Like the majestic mountains that surround us, the past and present converge in a dance of controversy, each step revealing a deeper truth. As the sun sets over the towering peaks, the question remains: what lies at the heart of this treacherous terrain, and how will we navigate its depths to emerge unscathed?
