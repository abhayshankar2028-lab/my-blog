As I sit here, sipping my lukewarm tea, I'm reminded of the fragility of our entertainment. Ready or Not 2, the sequel to this frightfully dull film, is a cinematic tapestry woven with threads of frustration. Like a well-written poem, the script is riddled with clichés and predictable twists, leaving the audience feeling like they're wandering through a dark forest, searching for a glimpse of light. The movie's attempt to up the ante with more gore and violence only serves to underscore the sense of monotony that pervades the entire experience. It's a slow-motion tragedy, a cautionary tale of how quickly a talented cast can go from brilliance to boredom. The result is a film that's more exhausting than exhilarating, a dull slog that fails to deliver on its promise of thrills and chills.
