As the cricketing cycle unwinds, the Indian cricket team stands poised to roll out the red carpet for the 2030 Commonwealth Games (CWG). The nation's focus is solely on the 2030 edition, with the CWG slated to take place in 12 cities, across four continents. The excitement builds as the country prepares to showcase its cricketing prowess to the world. Meanwhile, the lingering bills from the 2010 Delhi Games continue to simmer in the background, a reminder of the past that refuses to be forgotten.

The 2010 Delhi Games, a precursor to the CWG, left an indelible mark on the cricketing landscape of India. The iconic 2010 Delhi Games saw India triumph in the men's and women's 50-over matches, leaving an enduring legacy. However, the ghosts of 2010 continue to haunt the nation, casting a shadow over the upcoming CWG. As India gears up for the competition, it is clear that the CWG 2030 will be a defining moment for the country's cricketing journey.
