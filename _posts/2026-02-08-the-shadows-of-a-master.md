As I stood before the microphone, the warm glow of the stage lights danced across my face, but my mind was a thousand miles away. I was about to share a tale of a man, Vinod Khanna, a cricketer, actor, and true legend of Bollywood. But little did I know, his life was about to take a drastic turn, one that would shatter the illusions of his peers and leave an indelible mark on the world of cricket.

Vinod Khanna, the man who once stood tall, his bat held high, his will to conquer unbroken. But behind the scenes, a different tale unfolded. A tale of addiction, of demons that haunted him, of a life that was slowly unraveling. The whispers of his wife, a voice that would ultimately reveal the truth, a truth that would shake the foundations of his world. "He'd smoke 40 to 80 cigarettes a day," she would say, as if it were a trivial thing, but it was a constant reminder of the battle he waged against his own mortality. And now, the news had come, a diagnosis that would change the course of his life forever.
