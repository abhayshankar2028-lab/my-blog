Michael Jackson's life, a towering figure of music and magic, is the subject of the upcoming biopic, Michael. But, as I've watched this film, I couldn't help but feel that it's avoiding the darker aspects of his story. The cinematography, a poignant portrayal of his early days at Motown, is a beautiful tribute to the Jackson 5. However, the narrative, a hasty and unconvincing portrayal of the King of Pop's rise to fame, feels shrouded in a thick fog of gloss.

Like a shadowy figure lurking in the background, Michael Jackson's personal demons continue to haunt him, but his biopic fails to delve into the depths of these struggles. Instead, it presents a sanitized, feel-good narrative that leaves one feeling underwhelmed. It's as if the filmmakers have taken the essence of Michael's story and wrapped it in a neat, Hollywood bow, without ever truly exploring the complexities of his soul. A biopic that glosses over the darker side of Michael Jackson's life is a misstep, a missed opportunity to tell a truly epic story.
