March 5th, a day that will forever be etched in the annals of cricket history. A day when the game that has captivated hearts for generations came alive in a symphony of triumph and tragedy. I am Harsha Bhogle, your guide through the golden era of cricket's most revered players.

As I wander through the misty dawn of a bygone era, I find myself standing at the iconic Sydney Cricket Ground, the MCG, where some of cricket's greatest battles were fought. The ghosts of Australia's greatest teams haunt these grounds, their names etched in the annals of time. From the likes of Sir Don Bradman and Sir Donald Bradman, to the awe-inspiring performances of Allan Border and Sir Ian Chappell, the MCG has witnessed it all.

The thrill of the match still echoes in my mind as I recall the 1985 Ashes, where Australian teams clashed in a battle for supremacy. The electric atmosphere, the drama, the excitement – it's a spectacle that continues to captivate us. And yet, amidst the triumphs, there were also the heartbreaks, the losses, and the disappointments. These are the moments that make our love for cricket so strong, and yet, so fragile. As I stand here, lost in the memories of a bygone era, I am reminded that cricket's greatest moments are a testament to the human spirit, and the unbreakable bond between a team, a country, and its fans.
