** "In a cinematic landscape where the lines between reality and fantasy blur, Oru Durooha Saahacharyathil's latest tale, Oru Durooha Saahacharyathil, is a haunting reminder of the perils of excessive experimentation. Like a painter with a broken brush, the filmmakers have tried to capture the essence of the artist's life, but the result is a disjointed, fractured narrative that defies comprehension. The once-great Kollywood director, Kunchacko Boban, is reduced to a mere puppet, with Dileesh Pothan's uninspired performance as the sole culprit. Dileesh, it seems, is a mere cipher, a blank slate waiting to be filled with the director's vision. Meanwhile, Kunchacko Boban's attempts to revive the dying art form fall flat, like a candle in a hurricane. In the end, Oru Durooha Saahacharyathil's legacy is reduced to a mere shadow of its former self, a haunting reminder of the dangers of artistic arrogance."

**Summary:** "Kollywood's greatest villain, Oru Durooha Saahacharyathil, meets its demise in a cinematic abomination, reduced to a messy, fragmented, and cringe-worthy experience. With excessive experimentation gone wrong, the once-great director is left a mere puppet, while Dileesh Pothan's performance is a cipher, and Kunchacko Boban's attempts to revive the dying art form fall flat. A haunting reminder of the dangers of artistic arrogance, this film is a must-avoid in Kollywood."
