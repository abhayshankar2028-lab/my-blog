"I am not thinking about a comeback for India," he confesses, his voice a gentle breeze that whispers secrets to the wind.

The whispers speak of a nation's longing, a people's nostalgia for a bygone era. The memories of India's conquests, its triumphs, and its defeats, all swirl together, a maelstrom of emotions that threaten to upend the balance. Kumar's words are the first whispers of a storm, a gentle zephyr that hints at the tempests to come.

As the spotlight fades, the curtains draw back, revealing a crescendo of doubts and fears. Will India's players heed the warning signs, or will they continue to dance with the ghosts of their past?
