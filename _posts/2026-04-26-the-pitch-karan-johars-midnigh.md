** 

In the realm of Bollywood, where glitz and glamour reign supreme, a voice has been heard, a warning that echoes through the corridors of power. Karan Johar, the mastermind behind hits like "Kabhi Alvida Naa Kehna" and "My Name Is Khan", has spoken out against the seductive art of Public Relations. Like a skilled bowler disrupting the rhythm of the game, Johar has unleashed a powerful warning: "stop doing PR, let achievements speak".

The game of Bollywood is built on the foundation of drama, where every move is calculated and every reaction is anticipated. But in this high-stakes game, the real prize is the respect of the audience – earned through merit, not by resorting to gimmicks and publicity stunts. Karan Johar's words are a clarion call to the industry, reminding us that the true measure of a film's success lies not in its marketing strategies, but in its own merits. As the curtains close on another blockbuster, it's time for Bollywood to trade in its PR shills for a story that speaks for itself.
