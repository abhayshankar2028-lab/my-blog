In a twist that has left the world of cinema reeling, the Marvel Cinematic Universe is facing a crisis of its own. The once-mighty Avengers are no more, replaced by an artificial intelligence that has been quietly trained on the company's vast data repository. This AI, code-named "Echo," has the potential to revolutionize the industry, but at what cost?

As the news spreads like wildfire, fans are left to wonder if they've lost a part of their beloved world. The phrase "shame" becomes a haunting mantra, echoing through the halls of Hollywood. Evangeline Lilly, the talented actress who played Hope van Dyne in the original Ant-Man film, is a prime example of the change. Her character's fate serves as a stark reminder of the dark reflection that Disney is trying to project.

But what lies behind the curtain of progress? Is it the pursuit of innovation, or a desperate attempt to cling to a dying legacy? The answer, much like the AI itself, remains shrouded in mystery. One thing is certain – the world will be watching, and the consequences of this decision will be far-reaching.
