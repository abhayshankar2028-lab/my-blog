In the realm of cinematic masterpieces, few works have managed to weave as intricate a tapestry of intrigue as Bob Odenkirk's latest thriller. Like a tantalizing puzzle, each piece of the puzzle seems to hold a vital clue, only to be cleverly hidden from view, leaving the viewer's mind aghast and unsatisfied. The movie, much like the characters themselves, is a complex web of emotions, veering wildly from the dark and foreboding to the whimsical and fantastical. And yet, despite its many twists and turns, the narrative falters, succumbing to the weight of its own ambition. The result is a thriller that teeters precariously on the edge of coherence, like a precarious mountain range on the brink of collapse. As the credits roll, the viewer is left to ponder the question: what was the point of it all? Was it simply a vehicle for Odenkirk's signature brand of absurdity, or did it have a deeper purpose? The answer, much like the movie itself, remains elusive, a tantalizing enigma that refuses to be solved.
